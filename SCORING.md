# Macro Placement Challenge — Grand Prize Scoring Rules (v2.0)

This document defines the **deterministic, rules-based evaluation system** for the Grand Prize ($20,000).

The goal is simple:
- No ambiguity
- No subjective judging
- No post-hoc decisions

All submissions are evaluated using a **fixed pipeline** and **mechanical scoring rules**.

---

## Overview

The Grand Prize evaluation consists of **two stages**:

1. **Feasibility** — hard timing constraints (pass/fail)
2. **Scoring** — deterministic ranking via geometric mean of improvement ratios

Only submissions that pass **Stage 1** on **all designs** are eligible for the Grand Prize.

---

## Which Submissions Are Evaluated?

The **top 7 submissions by Tier 1 proxy cost** (IBM benchmarks) are evaluated through the full OpenROAD flow on NG45 designs.

Designs evaluated:
- `ariane133` (public)
- `ariane136` (public)
- `mempool_tile` (public)
- `nvdla` (public)
- 1–2 hidden NG45 designs (to prevent overfitting)

---

## Baselines

Baselines are generated by running **Simulated Annealing (SA)** and **RePlAce (RP)** through the **same OpenROAD evaluation flow** used for submissions.

For each design, we measure:
- **WNS** — Worst Negative Slack (ns). Higher (closer to 0) is better.
- **TNS** — Total Negative Slack (ns). Higher (closer to 0) is better.
- **Area** — Core area (μm²). Lower is better.

Baseline numbers will be published for all public designs once generated.

---

## Stage 1 — Feasibility (Timing Constraints)

A submission must **not regress timing** below both baselines on any design.

For each design:

```
WNS_sub ≥ min(WNS_SA, WNS_RP)
TNS_sub ≥ min(TNS_SA, TNS_RP)
```

### Interpretation

- You **cannot be worse than both baselines** on WNS or TNS for any design
- You may match one baseline and beat the other
- If either condition fails on **any design** → submission is **disqualified** from the Grand Prize

---

## Stage 2 — Scoring (Geometric Mean of Ratios)

All feasible submissions are ranked using a **single scalar score** computed from improvement ratios. This approach:

- Requires no arbitrary weights
- Naturally handles metrics on different scales
- Rewards **balanced improvement** across all three metrics

### Step 1 — Compute Average Baselines

For each design, compute the average of SA and RePlAce:

```
WNS_avg = (WNS_SA + WNS_RP) / 2
TNS_avg = (TNS_SA + TNS_RP) / 2
Area_avg = (Area_SA + Area_RP) / 2
```

### Step 2 — Compute Improvement Ratios

For each design:

```
R_WNS  = WNS_sub  / WNS_avg       (both values are negative; ratio > 1 means better)
R_TNS  = TNS_sub  / TNS_avg       (both values are negative; ratio > 1 means better)
R_Area = Area_avg / Area_sub      (lower area is better, so invert)
```

### Step 3 — Compute Per-Design Score

```
Design_Score = (R_WNS × R_TNS × R_Area) ^ (1/3)
```

- **Score > 1.0** → better than average baseline
- **Score = 1.0** → matched baseline exactly
- **Score < 1.0** → worse than average baseline

### Step 4 — Compute Total Score

```
Total_Score = geometric mean of Design_Scores across all evaluated designs
            = (Design_Score_1 × Design_Score_2 × ... × Design_Score_N) ^ (1/N)
```

**Highest Total Score wins the Grand Prize.**

### Example

Suppose for `ariane133`:
- SA baseline: WNS = -1.4 ns, TNS = -11,000 ns, Area = 4,300,000 μm²
- RePlAce baseline: WNS = -1.2 ns, TNS = -9,000 ns, Area = 4,100,000 μm²
- Average baseline: WNS = -1.3 ns, TNS = -10,000 ns, Area = 4,200,000 μm²
- Submission: WNS = -0.8 ns, TNS = -6,000 ns, Area = 4,000,000 μm²

```
R_WNS  = -0.8 / -1.3 = 0.615   → less negative = better, but ratio < 1?
```

**Wait — important note on WNS/TNS ratio interpretation:**

Since WNS and TNS are negative values (slack violations), a *less negative* value is better. When dividing two negative numbers, a less negative numerator (better) over a more negative denominator gives a ratio **less than 1**. To make the ratio intuitive (> 1 = better):

```
R_WNS  = WNS_avg / WNS_sub       (flipped: avg over submission)
R_TNS  = TNS_avg / TNS_sub       (flipped: avg over submission)
R_Area = Area_avg / Area_sub     (avg over submission)
```

Corrected example:
```
R_WNS  = -1.3 / -0.8  = 1.625   (62.5% better WNS)
R_TNS  = -10000 / -6000 = 1.667  (66.7% better TNS)
R_Area = 4200000 / 4000000 = 1.05 (5% smaller area)

Design_Score = (1.625 × 1.667 × 1.05) ^ (1/3) = (2.844) ^ (1/3) = 1.417
```

This submission scores **1.417** on ariane133 — roughly 42% better than the average baseline across all three metrics.

---

## Tie-Breaking

If two submissions have identical Total Scores:

1. Higher geometric mean of R_TNS across designs
2. Higher geometric mean of R_WNS across designs
3. Higher geometric mean of R_Area across designs

---

## Edge Cases

- **WNS or TNS = 0.0 for both baselines**: If both baselines achieve zero slack on a design, that metric's ratio is excluded from the geometric mean for that design (reduces to 2 metrics).
- **Submission achieves WNS/TNS = 0.0**: Capped at the best possible ratio (baseline / -0.001) to avoid division by zero.

---

## Disallowed Strategies

Submissions may be disqualified if they:

- Intentionally degrade timing to game scoring
- Modify or bypass the evaluation flow
- Hardcode design-specific hacks targeting known benchmarks

---

## Reproducibility

- Evaluation uses a **fixed, versioned OpenROAD flow**
- The evaluation pipeline will be released alongside final results
- All submissions are evaluated under **identical conditions** on the same hardware

---

## Summary

To win the Grand Prize:

1. Be in the **top 7 by proxy cost** (Tier 1)
2. **Pass feasibility** — don't regress timing below both baselines on any design
3. **Maximize your geometric mean score** — balanced improvement across WNS, TNS, and Area on all designs

This is a **strictly rules-based system**. There is **no subjective judgment** in ranking.
